Back in March, I wrote a list of [changes I wanted to make to my blog]({% post_url 2026-03-03-Tweaks %}).

To hold myself accountable, here are the changes I've made:

**#1. Scrollable sidebar.** The original [Hyde theme](https://github.com/poole/hyde) had a sticky sidebar. But after adding more items, it overflowed off the screen. Now it's scrollable. I can add as many items as I want. Muahahaha!

**#2. New tagline.** After [burning out]({% post_url 2025-01-04-RealizationsFrom2024 %}) and [diversifying my joy]({% post_url 2025-01-07-DiversifyYourJoy %}), I stopped being a "passionate" coder. These days, I'm a lifelong learner who codes part-time and [write most of the time]({% post_url 2026-04-19-WhyIWrite %}).

That's why I changed my tagline on the sidebar from simply _"Software engineer"_ to _"Sometimes I code, always I write."_ Cooler, isn't it? And it reflects what you will find here.

**#3. Better homepage and about copy.** My blog isn't a coding-only blog anymore. It's more like [a time capsule]({% post_url 2026-03-23-TimeCapsule %}). My [About page](/about) reflects that now...and I had some typos. So embarrassing, [calling myself a writer]({% post_url 2025-01-23-CallingYourselfAWriter %}).

And on my homepage, I'm only featuring [my blog](/blog) and [my books](/books). [No more Buy Me a Coffee]({% post_url 2025-07-07-AskingForCoffee %}) and Udemy courses. Less clutter.

That's the good part of personal blogs—and the bad part too. You're never done tweaking it. And if [you code and want to blog]({% post_url 2024-09-23-StrugglingToWrite %}), don't start by writing a blogging engine. Wait, did I say that out loud?
