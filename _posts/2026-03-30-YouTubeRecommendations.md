## #1. Fabio Cerpelloni's Tiny Books

A podcast interview with Seth Godin, the godfather of [daily blogging]([The Main Benefit of a Daily Writing Habit: 100 Daily Posts Reflection]({% post_url 2025-02-09-100DailyPosts %})), led me down a YouTube rabbit hole.

During the interview, they mentioned _The Practice_, one of Seth's books. Curious, I Googled it and found [Fabio Cerpelloni's channel](https://www.youtube.com/@fabiocerpelloni/videos).

He interviews writers, reviews books, and inspires others to write _tiny books_. His [questions to discover the book inside you](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mFR9QlM4Qg4) gave me ideas for at least two more books.

His videos feel like an unscripted conversation, like asking a friend about [writing books]({% post_url 2025-10-07-MiniBooks %}).

## #2. The Kings Hand

Doomscrolling is bad, right? It's a black hole for our time and attention.

The other day, [scrolling taught me a valuable life lesson]({% post_url 2025-07-29-InstagramLesson %}). And this time, I discovered a hidden gem about creativity, [The Kings Hand](https://www.youtube.com/@TheKingsHand1/videos).

This channel is about creativity in visual arts and music. Its official description is _cultural strategy and creative psychology breakdowns for ambitious creators._

Binge-watching The Kings Hand made me ask about my visual identity. A silhouette of a tuxedoed man leaning forward with his hand in his hat instantly recalls Michael Jackson. That's the power of visual identity.
