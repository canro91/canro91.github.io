Yesterday, a friend asked me, _"How does writing a book feel?"_
She had just bought a printed copy of [10 Simple Ideas That Changed My Life]({% post_url 2026-03-04-10SimpleIdeas %}).

Well...

The first days are full of excitement.
I use [10-idea lists]({% post_url 2025-06-17-TenIdeas %}) to come up with a title, subtitle, and outline.
I come up with cover ideas.

The next few days are full of doubt.
It's when [the inner critic speaks louder]({% post_url 2026-06-18-InnerCritic %}).
_"Is anyone going to like this?"_

The next few weeks are quiet.
Like a marathon, it's one foot after the other.
It's doing something small every day.

[The first draft]({% post_url 2026-02-09-FirstDraft %}) brings accomplishment—and some anxiety.
_"What are my beta readers going to say?"_
_"Wait! Is this really worth publishing?"_

The roller coaster peaks when the first printed copies arrive.
Opening that box of books is pure satisfaction.
_"This was in my head and now it's in my hands."_

But the ride always goes down.
The temptation is to refresh sales daily.
_"Why isn't this book selling? Is it the price? The description?"_

That's when I let go of control.
Time to start thinking about the next project.

It's a roller coaster, worth every ride.
A ride I'll keep taking, turning parts of [my life into books]({% post_url 2026-02-09-FirstDraft %}).
