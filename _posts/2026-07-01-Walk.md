From Derek Sivers' [Useful But Not True]({% post_url 2026-05-23-UsefulButNotTrue %}), you can always choose your next thought or reaction.

But yesterday, I failed to choose my next reaction after a frustrating purchase over the phone.
Then I hit a roadblock with a coding task.
I felt how my energy was depleted.

To change my mood, I switched to [writing, something I enjoy]({% post_url 2026-04-19-WhyIWrite %}).

Hoping to find something to steal, I opened my RSS reader and checked [my favorite blog aggregators]({% post_url 2026-04-25-Newspaper %}).
But scrolling only made things worse.
It ended up draining the remaining energy.
[Screens don't energize me]({% post_url 2026-05-18-AnalogSundays %}).

Until it was time for my daily walk.

Fresh air, the outdoors, and oxygenated blood always lift a bad mood, just like finding a health package in a video game.

A walk can fix almost anything, by giving you fresh eyes to approach it.
