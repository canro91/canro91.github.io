## Nah... I don't care

In early 2023, a coworker tried that new thing called ChatGPT.
He shared his excitement.
I wasn't that impressed.

Months later, after all the buzz, I searched that _Chat thing_.
Every time I tried, I couldn't access it.
It was a public beta or something.
Too many people.

Then, I learned from [Brent Ozar]({% post_url 2025-02-02-LessonsFromBrentOzar %}) to keep a browser tab with ChatGPT open as a junior assistant.
_Mmm, there's something there..._

In early 2024, [Devin was released]({% post_url 2024-04-29-2034Predictions %}).
"The sky is falling" all over the headlines.
I didn't buy it.

## OMG! This is dangerous!

Early 2025, I decided to try AI with a fresh mind.

After using Copilot for weeks, _"OMG! This thing is dangerous."_
One day I realized I couldn't finish a simple task alone.
The tool worked, but [I was becoming so dependent]({% post_url 2025-07-13-TheProblemWithAI %}).

An AI detox season followed.
_No more AI until you eat your vegetables and do your homework._

Then I made [some]({% post_url 2025-10-14-AIRule %}) [rules]({% post_url 2026-01-26-AnotherAIRule %}) to use AI without losing my skills.

Like any other tool, AI is a game changer with the right skills.
Otherwise, [it's like a fancy calculator]({% post_url 2025-11-27-HowToThinkOfAI %}) in a math exam you didn't study for.

To thrive in the AI era, [build real skills, then leverage AI]({% post_url 2026-02-12-RealSkills %}).

To help you build AI-proof skills, I wrote [Street-Smart Coding](https://imcsarag.gumroad.com/l/streetsmartcoding?utm_source=blog&utm_medium=post&utm_campaign=my-new-book-is-here). The guide I wish I had on my journey from junior to senior.
