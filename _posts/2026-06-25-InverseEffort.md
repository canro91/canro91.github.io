_The less time spent tweaking a post, the more it seems to resonate._

Yesterday, I hit [the 600 daily post milestone]({% post_url 2026-06-24-600DailyPosts %}).

The most popular posts were often the ones I spent less time editing.
Some were only to [avoid breaking the chain]({% post_url 2024-12-14-Consistency %}).
The ones I poured my soul into resonated less.

Same observation after [300+ LinkedIn posts]({% post_url 2025-06-15-LinkedIn %}).

I'm calling it: _the inverse effort effect._

Too much tweaking dilutes the real message and hides [your true voice]({% post_url 2025-03-05-WritingVoice %}).
Maybe that's why rants [go viral]({% post_url 2025-02-05-LessonsFromAViralPost %}) and invite replies.
They feel raw and authentic.

From the _inverse effort effect_, not every post is a winner.
But unread posts still count: as practice, as a data point, as one [time capsule entry]({% post_url 2026-03-23-TimeCapsule %}).
Definitely not time wasted.

I'll stop here before I fall prey to this effect.
