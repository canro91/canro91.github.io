Creativity begins with imitation.

We transcribe a passage from our favorite writer.
We recreate a scene from our favorite painter.
We first adopt, then adapt.

I've hand-copied [James Altucher]({% post_url 2024-11-11-LessonsFromJamesAltucher %})'s passages and [dissected Dan Brown's bestsellers]({% post_url 2026-05-12-Flashbacks %}).

But don't only [steal the work]({% post_url 2025-11-08-StealLikeAnArtist %}), steal the process behind it too.

Watch how your favorite writer [launches a book]({% post_url 2025-11-22-HardTruthsWritingABook %}).
Or how a painter curates a gallery.

After [studying Craig Mod's membership]({% post_url 2026-05-26-HorizonGoal %}), I wrote [10-idea lists]({% post_url 2025-06-17-TenIdeas %}) for my own using my current setup.

As a creative constraint, replicate their production with [what you have]({% post_url 2026-04-28-YourRoomAndHead %}), even with $0.
Steal their art and production.
