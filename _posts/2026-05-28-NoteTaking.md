Inspired by Herman's idea of [resurfacing old posts](https://herman.bearblog.dev/resurfacing-posts/), here are some of my old posts on note-taking:

**#1.** Back in 2020, I learned about the _Zettelkasten_ method.
I was so intrigued that I read [How to Take Smart Notes]({% post_url 2020-11-18-HowToTakeSmartNotes %}), probably the most popular book on the subject.

**#2.** Being a plain text lover, I started [my own perverted digital version]({% post_url 2024-05-13-HowToReadNonFictionBooks %}).
Instead of using separate "literature" and "main" notes, I kept them in the same file.

I realized I had notes of books I didn't remember reading.
I was collecting notes without purpose. Just a hoarder.
Eventually, I stopped using the method.

**#3.** After learning about being an Idea Machine, I [ditched my second brain]({% post_url 2025-06-22-SimplerNoteTaking %}) and started to write 10 ideas after reading a book.

**#4.** More recently, as an experiment, I decided to [start a physical Zettelkasten]({% post_url 2026-04-14-Zettelkasten %}).
As a creative constraint, [I considered writing a book only from my notes]({% post_url 2026-04-28-YourRoomAndHead %}).

**#5.** Looking for a how-to book, I found [How to Make Notes and Write]({% post_url 2026-05-06-HowToMakeNotesAndWrite %}).
A simpler approach: two types of notes—source and point—focused on writing as output.
