**#1. Always have something to write on.** This week, I caught up with an ex-coworker. He shared a valuable story that deserved [its own post]({% post_url 2026-01-27-ExpertAtFailing %}). Thank goodness I grabbed a napkin from the cafe where we met. That's where I outlined the post.

**#2. The notebook cult.** Finding out about zines to [reduce my phone time]({% post_url 2026-01-22-LessScrolling %}) took me to a YouTube rabbit hole. Turns out there's a whole cult. People with notebooks for everything. Notebooks for quotes, journaling, habit trackers, commonplace notebooks, pocket notebooks. I'm not joining the cult, I'm fine with [my idea pad]({% post_url 2024-11-05-IdeaMachine %}).

**#3. Start your next creative project backwards.** Before starting, imagine it's finished, then write the sales page and announcements. That forces you to clarify your goal and message. That's what I'm doing for my next book.

**#4. A simple method to overcome burnout.** A pen pal shared his burnout was appearing again. When he asked how I avoid burnout, I shared my mantra: care for your body, mind, and spirit daily. That's [one of the ideas that has changed my life]({% post_url 2025-07-03-LifeChangingIdeas %}).

**#5. 1, 2, 3, and get up.** I can't remember what podcast I learned this from. As soon as you wake up, count up to 3 and get up. Those moments after waking up are when you [become a time traveler]({% post_url 2024-12-19-TimeTravel %}), reliving the past and sketching possible futures.
