In 2023, after burning out, I felt completely lost.

[A daily wellness routine]({% post_url 2025-05-13-PrioritizeHealth %}) to care for my body, mind, and spirit saved me.
[That simple idea changed my life]({% post_url 2026-03-04-10SimpleIdeas %}).
I still follow it today.
No excuses.

Inspiring people helped [my burnout recovery]({% post_url 2025-08-11-Present %}).
Hearing and reading how others got back up truly helped.

That proved me it was possible to feel good after burnout.
Getting out of bed just felt hard.

Instead of doomscrolling, I filled my days reading from:
* [James Altucher]({% post_url 2024-11-11-LessonsFromJamesAltucher %}) and his bankrupt stories
* [Dan Koe]({% post_url 2025-06-01-DanKoe %}) and his "you're the niche" idea
* Borja Vilaseca and his self-discovery after a suicide attempt

I looked up to them.

More recently, I've added [Seth Godin]({% post_url 2025-02-09-100DailyPosts %}) and [Derek Sivers]({% post_url 2026-04-04-DerekSivers %}).

They had something in common:
* [Reinvention]({% post_url 2025-09-23-Reinvention %})
* Living on their own terms
* "Eating what they hunt"
* [Writing]({% post_url 2025-04-01-WhyIWillKeepWriting %})
* Books

Now that I stop to think about it, that's the path I'm trying to follow today.

When lost, find the thread between your heroes.
